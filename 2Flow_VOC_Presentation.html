In almost every case a specific event forced the decision - not a gradual drift.

Topic 7

Peak Performance

Peak performance is the single biggest source of both trust and friction. When 2Flow absorbs a peak well, loyalty deepens. When it doesn’t - two accounts can confirm it didn’t in 2024 - the relationship comes under serious strain that takes months to recover. The variability is the problem.

“There was one particular weekend where I was checking on dispatch - no people dispatching. This is the week before Black Friday.”

- Danielle, Spotlight Oral Care

Details
1
When peaks go well, customers feel safe and become stronger advocates
“A lot of the time the guys will still dispatch a 2pm order after the cutoff. The customer expected Wednesday - but finds the package delivered on Tuesday, and writes a review.” - Darren, Probiotic.ie
2
When peaks break down, the damage is significant and lingers
“Some of our online orders took up to two weeks to get to customers. We have a three to five day turnaround promise. We were snowed under for four weeks.” - Susanna O’Connor, Poco Beauty
“This year was a little bit more disappointing... there was one particular weekend where I was checking on dispatch and we had no people dispatching. This is the week before Black Friday.” - Danielle, Spotlight Oral Care
3
The ’which channel do you want to prioritise?’ question is commercially unacceptable
“We were being asked - what do you want to prioritise, retail or online? For any business during that peak period, you can imagine it’s not an ideal scenario.” - Danielle, Spotlight Oral Care
4
Weekend cover during peak is a structural gap customers have noticed
“Two of the guys had gone sick, so there was a delay. Maybe train a couple more people on brands so that you’re not relying on only two people - especially in peak season.” - Sinead, Ormonde Jayne
“I think it comes down to staff... separate teams for retail as opposed to online, so that both sides are being catered for equally.” - Susanna O’Connor, Poco Beauty

Topic 8

Demand Predictability & Stock

The most consistent daily frustration across the customer base is the unpredictability of stock check-in timelines. Customers are not asking for faster - they are asking to know how long it will take so they can plan. The absence of any expectation forces manual workarounds, customer service escalations, and product availability problems.

“Even if I know my deliveries are going to take a week to check in, I can forecast for that. But sometimes it’s really quick and sometimes it takes a really, really long time - and I just don’t know.”

- Emma, Skin Shop

Details
1
Most customers share some forecast data - but the quality varies significantly
“Probably not. I don’t think we’re very good on our side at maybe communicating when our sales are on or what’s a big weekend.” - Emma, Skin Shop
“I always try to share projections with them, especially if we are launching a new product, expecting more volume, if we are preparing for Black Friday or a sale.” - Mihael, Ancestral Cosmetics
“We did try and tell them how busy we were going to be, but I do think they slightly underestimated.” - Susanna O’Connor, Poco Beauty
2
Stock check-in timelines are unpredictable - customers need transparency, not perfection
“Cash flow means we can’t afford to order six months in advance. So at the minute I’m waiting for stock to be checked in that’s been in the warehouse for a week and a half.” - Emma, Skin Shop
“It only showed up on the system yesterday - eight days after the production order was placed.” - Priya, Unicity
3
Kitting and pre-build jobs are particularly hard to track
“We never could get them to tally what they said was done and what was done. After Christmas, 521 boxes were found - we always knew the quantities didn’t match.” - Susanna O’Connor, Poco Beauty
“Give me an expectation. Say, oh Emma - this week it’s hectic. Could we just maybe break this down into stages?” - Emma, Skin Shop
4
ASN errors compound the problem when inbound stock is partially or incorrectly booked
“ASNs sometimes aren’t booked in - or partially booked in - and you’re not told if there are pieces missing.” - Susanna O’Connor, Poco Beauty
“Please do not upload - check the discrepancy first. And what we get back is: stop, it’s been uploaded. Not even an apology.” - Danielle, Spotlight Oral Care

Topic 8

Demand Predictability
& Stock

Opportunities

Four actions to remove the most widely felt daily frustration across the current customer base - improving trust and reducing manual workarounds.

1
Publish and enforce a stock check-in SLA with proactive exception communication. Give clients a timeline. When exceptions arise, message them first - do not wait to be chased.
2
Introduce a structured monthly forecasting template for clients: expected inbound, anticipated peak dates, kitting jobs in the queue. The act of asking forces forward planning on both sides.
3
Fix kitting job visibility. Clients need a status view: queued, in progress, complete. Partial batch uploads create phantom inventory problems.
4
Resolve the kitting completion upload process with Mintsoft - completed jobs should be uploaded as a single batch with a timestamp.

Software friction came up in six of nine interviews and traces back to three problems: Shopify-Mintsoft dual entry for order changes, no visibility into kitting and stock status, and customers never being shown how to use the system to its potential. These are all fixable.

“At least an eight. If their system could link to ours, it would be a 10.”

- Cecilia, Coloplast

Details
1
Shopify-Mintsoft dual entry is the most cited software frustration across the base
“Whenever a customer wants to update the address, we just do it in Shopify and it syncs immediately. In Mintsoft, you need to change it in both systems.” - Mihael, Ancestral Cosmetics
“Whenever a customer wants to update the order, we will have to alert 2Flow, they make the amendment on Mintsoft, and then we have to make a separate amendment on Shopify so that we reference it.” - Susanna O’Connor, Poco Beauty
2
Priority order flagging is missing - express orders get lost in the standard queue
“There’s a way on the system for them to flag the express deliveries and pull them first, because they are priority. If the customer has paid a premium to have them next day, they should go first.” - Susanna O’Connor, Poco Beauty
“Those back orders, when they are released, they just get mixed into the orders that arrived that day. I would really like those orders to be at the top of the list.” - Cecilia, Coloplast
3
Inventory reporting and kitting tracking are not reliable enough to plan around
“They send us an inventory at the end of every month, but it’s not a true inventory if there’s stock sitting on the floor that hasn’t been booked in yet, or partially.” - Susanna O’Connor, Poco Beauty
4
Customers want Mintsoft training - and assume 2Flow would provide it if asked
“I probably would need a bit more training to know how to pull a report and get it really quickly. But I know that’s available. I just probably have never approached them to do that.” - Sinead, Ormonde Jayne
“We had training at the start. Before that, that was more for specific questions I had, but otherwise I was able to just use it for what I needed quite easily.” - Priya, Unicity

Issues & Friction Points

Friction is concentrated in five areas: stock check-in unpredictability, peak resourcing, defensive communication when problems arise, kitting job visibility, and software dual-entry. None individually fatal - but in combination they are eroding confidence in the accounts that matter most commercially.

“The thought process needs to change. It needs to be: hey, let us go and find a solution for you. Is that not what we get anymore?”

- Danielle, Spotlight Oral Care

“I don’t know when or how long certain things are going to take. Even if I know it will take a week, then I can forecast for that.” - Emma, Skin Shop
“Cash flow means we can’t afford to order six months in advance. So at the minute I’m waiting for stock to be checked in that’s been in the warehouse for a week and a half.” - Emma, Skin Shop

“We were being asked - what do you want to prioritise, retail or online? For any business during that peak period, you can imagine it’s not an ideal scenario.” - Danielle, Spotlight Oral Care
“I think it comes down to staff... separate teams for retail as opposed to online, so that both sides are being catered for equally.” - Susanna O’Connor, Poco Beauty

Priority 3 - Defensive communication when problems arise (Danielle, Susanna, Mihael)
“I just literally get a one-line back to say: stop, it’s been uploaded. Not - apologies, should have flagged.” - Danielle, Spotlight Oral Care
“The thought process needs to change. It needs to be: hey, let us go and find a solution for you. Is that not what we get anymore?” - Danielle, Spotlight Oral Care

Priority 4 - Kitting job visibility and tracking (Susanna, Emma, Danielle)
